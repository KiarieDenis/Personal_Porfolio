My Journey

Building Foundations.

I began my academic journey at Mama Ngina Primary School, where I built the foundations that still shape how I think today: discipline, curiosity, and consistency. This was where I first learned how to learn—how to follow structure, respect process, and stay persistent even when progress felt slow. Moving on to Gathiru-ini Secondary School, my thinking matured from absorbing information to questioning it. I became more analytical, more independent, and more aware that progress comes from understanding systems, not memorizing outcomes. Secondary education sharpened my resilience, time management, and responsibility for my own growth.

I continued to Sigalagala National Polytechnic, where theory began to meet real-world application. This stage transformed my mindset from that of a student to that of a problem solver. I complemented my academic training with paramilitary training at NYS Kenya, which instilled discipline, mental toughness, teamwork, and the ability to operate under pressure—skills that translate directly into engineering and high-responsibility environments. During my industrial attachment at Nairobi Water and Sewerage Company Ltd, I gained exposure to large-scale operational systems, learning how infrastructure, data, and human coordination intersect in mission-critical services. This experience grounded my technical interests in real societal impact.

I later worked at M-Gas Limited, where I learned how structured operations, accountability, and efficiency drive scalable services. Seeing technology-enabled systems support thousands of users reinforced my desire to build solutions that scale beyond individuals. I am currently a data science and ML engineering student at ALX, where my focus is on programming, automation, Data, analytics and applied artificial intelligence. At this stage, my journey converges into a clear direction: I am Denis Kiarie, a Data Science developer in the making with real-world industry exposure, disciplined training, and a systems-oriented mindset. I build with intent, learn with depth, and aim to design intelligent, scalable systems that create leverage, solve real problems, and continue working long after manual effort stops.
